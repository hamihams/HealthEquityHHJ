Patient Privacy and Confidentiality


Patients can be afraid that their health information and anything that makes them identifiable will be talked about with other healthcare staff or people in general without their consent.



However, the HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) Law, which is a law all healthcare staff have to follow, makes it a crime to leak any medical records and other personal healthcare information to other people that the patient has not given consent to.


Those who violate this crime can face

fine of up to $250,000, as well as imprisonment up to 10 years.
